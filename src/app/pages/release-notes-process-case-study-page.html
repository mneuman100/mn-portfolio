Observation

Technical product managers (TPMs) were missing SME information deadlines on tickets, especially in the last sprints of a release, which left the technical writer scrambling to finish their release notes by the documentation deadlines.

Method

I built a ticket tracking spreadsheet across a full release cycle to identify where deadline failures were occurring. Through retrospective sessions with TPMs, I discovered the documentation timeline was out of sync with development sprint cycles and deadline information wasn't visible in the tools TPMs used daily.

Using "How Might We" (HMW) questions, I synthesized these findings into four problem statements.

"How Might We" questions

  1. How Might We increase awareness of SME information deadlines?
  2. HMW increase awareness of tickets that need SME information?
  3. HMW make it easier for TPMs to complete tickets before the deadline?
  4. HMW ensure the technical writer has what they need, even when tickets are incomplete?
  5. HMW help the TW identify at-risk tickets early enough to follow up before the deadline?
  6. HMW make the documentation deadline visible to everyone involved in the sprint?

Scoped out and why

  1. HMW increase the ease of TPMS adding SME information to the ticket?
    • Duplicate of another, which is worded better
  2. HMW help SMEs determine the right information needed by the TW?
    • may be out of scope
  3. HMW find ways to help the SME mark the ticket earlier in the sprint cycle?
    • Out of scope

Using HMW questions, I brainstormed broad, open-ended opportunities, then crossed out ones that were out of scope.

Problem statements

  1. Flora, a product manager, needs a way to keep track of tickets that need SME information because she keeps missing tickets toward the end of the release.
  2. Flora, a product manager, needs to know exactly what information is required on each ticket before the sprint ends because incomplete tickets can't be processed, causing downstream delays for the technical writer.
  3. Sam, a technical writer, needs a way to identify at-risk tickets early because late SME information forces last-minute scrambling that threatens documentation deadlines.
  4. Flora, a product manager, and Sam, a technical writer, need documentation deadlines to be visible within their existing sprint workflow because deadline information currently lives outside the tools both use daily.

Insight

There was a visibility issue. TPMs didn't have a clear view of the deadline dates or what tickets needed to be completed.

Results

By adding widgets that showed what tickets needed TPM assistance on the existing technical writer dashboard, TPMs could now quickly see the status of their tickets.

By adjusting the documentation deadlines to be in sync with the pre-prod release schedule and requesting the dates get added to the release calendar, TPMs could now clearly see the deadline on their work calendars. Both solutions lead to less friction for release note completion and a reduction in tickets missing their documentation deadline from 29.3% to 21.6% in the very next release cycle.
