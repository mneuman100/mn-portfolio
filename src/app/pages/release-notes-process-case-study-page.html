Observation

At Origami Risk, Technical Product Managers (TPMs) were missing Subject Matter Expert (SME) information deadlines on tickets, especially in the last sprints of a release, which left the technical writer scrambling to finish their release notes by the documentation deadlines.

Method

I built a ticket tracking spreadsheet across a full release cycle to identify where deadline failures were occurring. Through retrospective sessions with TPMs, I discovered the documentation timeline was out of sync with development sprint cycles and deadline information wasn't visible in the tools TPMs used daily. Using "How Might We" (HMW) questions, I synthesized these findings into four problem statements.

Using HMW questions, I brainstormed broad, open-ended opportunities, then crossed out ones that were out of scope.

Insight

There was a visibility issue. TPMs didn't have a clear view of the deadline dates or what tickets needed to be completed.

Results

By adding widgets that showed what tickets needed TPM assistance on the existing technical writer dashboard, TPMs could now quickly see the status of their tickets.

By adjusting the documentation deadlines to be in sync with the pre-production release schedule and requesting the dates get added to the release calendar, TPMs could now clearly see the deadline on their work calendars. Both solutions lead to less friction for release note completion and a reduction in tickets missing their documentation deadline from 29.3% to 21.6% in the very next release cycle.
